Ready for more detailed planning?

Simple Mode keeps things clean by assuming your working CPF contributions perfectly offset your mortgage. Switch to Advanced Mode to customize exactly how your wealth is modeled:

  • Floating Mortgages: Use your remaining principal and current interest rate to dynamically model amortization and SORA rate swings.
  • Currency Risk: Map the long-term trend of the SGD appreciating against the USD.
  • CPF OA Shielding: Track your actual OA balance and salary contributions to see how long your CPF can shield your portfolio from the mortgage in retirement.
  • Monte Carlo Engine: Stress-test your timeline with randomized market crashes based on your portfolio's specific empirical volatility and extreme tail risks.

How to Use This Simulator & Assumptions Guide

Lines of Defense (Mortgage Shortfalls)

During your working years, if floating interest rates cause your mortgage payment to spike above your CPF OA Target, the simulator defends your cash buffer using a specific sequence:

  • Line 1 (CPF Buffer): It will drain any available excess balance in your CPF OA.
  • Line 2 (Reduce Investments): It will automatically reduce your monthly out-of-pocket investment contributions. It does this proportionally. For example, if you contribute 80% to Global and 20% to SG, the reduction will hit those portfolios at an 80/20 split.
  • Line 3 (Drain Cash): Only if the rate spike completely consumes all of your monthly investment contributions will it begin to drain your sacred Cash Reserve.

Floating Mortgages & Amortization (Advanced Mode)

Instead of a flat monthly payment, Advanced Mode calculates your mortgage dynamically using standard loan amortization. If you run a Monte Carlo simulation, your interest rate will fluctuate annually, which will immediately recalculate your required monthly payment based on your remaining principal and tenure.

FX Drift & Currency Risk (Advanced Mode)

Because the MAS uses exchange rate policy to manage inflation, the SGD has historically appreciated against the USD over the long term. The "Annual FX Drift" input allows you to model this depreciation of the US Dollar, which gradually reduces the local SGD purchasing power of your global portfolio over decades.

Architecture & Withdrawals in Retirement

When you retire and your expenses exceed your income, capital is drawn down in this strict order to maximize compounding time for high-yield assets: 1. Cash Reserve -> 2. SGD Portfolio -> 3. USD Portfolio.

⚠️ Limitations & Blindspots (For Beginners)

This tool provides a strong baseline, but real life is messier than math. Do not base major life decisions on this chart without understanding its blindspots:

  • The "Stagflation" Blindspot: The macroeconomic preset buttons pair lower market returns with higher inflation. However, real life can be much harsher. "Stagflation" occurs when inflation spikes dramatically at the exact same time markets crash (e.g., 2022 or the 1970s). The presets do not model this double-impact; you must manually type in high inflation and negative returns to stress-test a true stagflation environment.
  • The Cash Drawdown Trap: The simulator strictly drains your cash reserve first before touching equities. While mathematically optimal, in reality, you should maintain a cash buffer to avoid selling stocks during a market crash.
  • Invisible Tax Drag: If your global portfolio includes US-domiciled ETFs, remember that the US levies a 30% Dividend Withholding Tax on Singaporeans. A projected 8% gross return might effectively be 7.4% after this tax drag.
  • The Retirement "Smile": The tool inflates expenses on a straight line. Real retirement spending usually "smiles"—high in early retirement (travel), lower in the middle, and spiking heavily at the end due to healthcare/nursing costs.
  • CPF Simplification: At age 55, the tool deducts your entire Retirement Account (RA) set-aside from your OA. In reality, your Special Account (SA) funds this first, meaning your actual OA might not drop as drastically as simulated here.
  • Monte Carlo Limits: Unless the "Black Swan" fat-tailed risk is enabled, the simulation assumes market returns follow a standard "bell curve." A standard normal distribution systematically underestimates the frequency of massive, multi-year market crashes.
